About

My name is Ivan Hanigan. I am a PhD student in the National Centre for Epidemiology and Population Health (NCEPH) at the Australian National University (ANU).

I am a multidisciplinary data manager and analyst with over 10 years experience at one of Australia's leading universities. I have primarily worked in Environmental Epidemiology where I have honed my skills in manipulation of large databases and multivariate regression modelling. I also have experience of scenario-based forecasting in climate change risk assessments.

I have been the lead author on four papers in international journals, and contributing author on numerous journal papers, reports and conference presentations.

I have used the Data Documentation Initiative (DDI) international standard for metadata since 2009 to ensure the data management system is suitably documented and maintained throughout the Data Analysis Lifecycle.

My programming expertise is primarily with the R language for statistical computing and graphics but I also am adept programming and configuring Postgres, PostGIS, Geoserver, QGIS, ArcGIS, Stata, SPSS, Oracle-XE, Linux (Ubuntu, Centos, RHEL).

Feel free to contact me ivan dot hanigan at gmail dot com
