Biography

I am a multidisciplinary data manager and analyst with over 10 years experience at one of Australia’s leading universities. I have primarily worked in Environmental Epidemiology where I have honed my skills in manipulation of large databases and multivariate regression modelling. I also have experience of scenario-based forecasting in climate change risk assessments.

The integration of gridded, point-source and area-based administrative data has become a speciality. I have experience in the integration of health, social and environmental data, in particular reconciling health and population data based on incompatible spatial units, and the creation of and linkage with environmental exposure maps. I am adept in the management of large databases as well as statistical techniques such as Generalised Linear Modelling and Decision Tree analysis. I have been the lead author on four papers in international journals, and contributing author on numerous journal papers, reports and conference presentations.

I am also undertaking a multi-disciplinary PhD, on a part time basis.
